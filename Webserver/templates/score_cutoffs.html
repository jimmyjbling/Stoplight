STOPLIGHT: Descriptions of Stoplight Score

Like golf, the STOPLIGHT scores are inverted, ei smaller scores are better, larger are worse. This score is an equal weighted average of all scores assigned to a predicted/calculated properties. For any individual property a discrete score of 0, 1, or 2 is assigned based on a series of established cutoffs gathered from literature and standard practices (see below). STOPLIGHT will assign a traffic color to a compound following the below cutoffs for the overall average:

0 1 2
Green Yellow Red

Note that only properties turned on in the model options sidebar will be considered when calculating scores. This can change the score of compounds if different properties are considered.

Only molecular properties and assay liabilities are scored. This is due to PK property values generally having campaign specific goals. You can assign your own weighting using the output CSV if desired

Molecular property scores

The cutoffs for the six molecular properties are gathered from literature. They are enumerated below. Scores can be 0, 1, or 2. 0 means no penalty, 1 is minor penalty and 2 is major penalty. All 6 properties are weighted equally
Property 0 1 2
Solubility in water >=50 10-50 <10
LogP <2 2-3 >3
Molecular Weight <=400 400-500 >500
PSA <=120 120-140 >140
Num rotatable bonds <=7 8-10 >=11
FSP3 >0.3 0.2-0.3 <0.2

Assay liability scores

Some compounds can interfere with assay reporting, making them assay liabilities. STOPLIGHT can predict if compounds are liabilities using models published in literature. Currently, models exist for 6 types of interference. Note that these are predictions, and their accuracy is not 100% (see original publication for assessment of accuracy). As a result, compounds with predicted liability will be scored as a 1, and compounds without scored as a 0. Currently, compounds cannot be scored a 2 in this category.

A consensus liability score is generated by averaging each liability model's predicted probability that given compound is a liability. Thus, just like the STOPLIGHT score this score is inverted; a consensus score near or at 0 means no liability and a score near or at 1 liability.
